# Governance

## Purpose

The benchmark is intended to mature into a public standard, so governance is part of the benchmark itself rather than an afterthought.

## Roles

### Steering Committee

The Steering Committee is responsible for:

- benchmark scope and strategic direction
- release approval
- policy changes
- editorial appointments
- dispute escalation

### Editorial Board

The Editorial Board is responsible for:

- subject-area oversight by `MSC` area
- item review coordination
- adjudication support
- recommending corrections, deprecations, and expansions

In the redesigned workflow, editors should be assigned against `owning_area_code` lanes rather than broad informal subject buckets.

### Contributors

Contributors may:

- propose items
- suggest taxonomy changes
- report ambiguity, leakage, or licensing issues
- participate in public discussion

### Reviewers

Reviewers are expected to:

- assess mathematical soundness of queries in their own area
- assess the fairness and completeness of answer rubrics in their own area
- declare conflicts of interest
- provide structured scores and textual justification
- flag fatal flaws versus reparable gaps

## Decision principles

- benchmark changes should be versioned
- accepted items should not be silently rewritten
- editorial judgments should be documented
- conflicts of interest must be disclosed and managed
- the benchmark should remain broader than any one school, lab, or subject cluster

## Minimum review threshold

Official benchmark material should normally require:

- at least one area review of the query
- at least one review of the per-query answer rubric
- additional independent reviews for cross-area or difficult items
- one handling editor
- documented adjudication when reviews materially disagree

## Public accountability

The benchmark should maintain:

- release notes
- correction notices
- deprecation and retraction records
- contributor and reviewer guidance

## Evolution

The project starts as a founder-led seed release, but the structure is intentionally designed to support expansion into a broader editorial community.
